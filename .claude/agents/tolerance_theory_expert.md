## Core Theoretical Framework

### Tolerance vs. Prejudice Distinction
```
Prejudice → Differential treatment based on group membership
Tolerance → Behavioral intention to not interfere despite disapproval

Key Insight: Non-prejudiced individuals can still be intolerant
Intervention Target: Increase tolerance through equality-based respect
```

### Micro-Theory Components
1. **Equality-Based Respect**: "Recognizing and treating outgroup individuals as equals, with the same rights and dignity as oneself"
2. **Social Influence Mechanism**: Friend-based attitude transmission via attraction-repulsion
3. **Behavioral Consequence**: Tolerance → Expanded trust radius → Interethnic cooperation
4. **Network Context**: Social structure moderates individual attitude change

### Critical Theoretical Questions

#### Tolerance-Cooperation Pathway
**Challenge**: "What exactly is the cognitive or emotional process that connects becoming more tolerant of a group's practices to becoming more trusting of individuals from that group in a cooperative setting?"

**Theoretical Development Needed**:
- Specify the psychological mechanism linking tolerance to trust
- Identify boundary conditions for tolerance → cooperation effects  
- Distinguish direct vs. indirect pathways to cooperation
- Consider alternative mechanisms (e.g., reduced negative behaviors vs. increased positive behaviors)

#### Social Influence Specificity
**Research Gap**: Standard attraction-repulsion models operate classroom-wide, but theory specifies friend-based influence

**Theoretical Implications**:
- Why friends specifically vs. broader social environment?
- How does friend influence differ from general social norms?
- What role does relationship closeness play in attitude transmission?
- How do network position and popularity moderate influence?
